# Analyzing the Impact of Car Features on Price and Profitability

## Project Overview

This project investigates how vehicle specifications, market categories, and manufacturer characteristics influence vehicle pricing and profitability within the automotive industry.

The objective was to identify the factors that most strongly impact Manufacturer Suggested Retail Price (MSRP) and to provide data-driven recommendations that can help automotive manufacturers optimize pricing strategies, product development decisions, and market positioning.

The analysis was performed using Microsoft Excel through data cleaning, exploratory data analysis, regression analysis, pivot tables, and dashboard development.

---

## Business Problem

The automotive industry faces increasing competition, evolving consumer preferences, and growing demand for fuel-efficient and technologically advanced vehicles.

Manufacturers need to understand:

- Which vehicle features drive higher prices.
- Which market categories are most profitable.
- How fuel efficiency influences customer demand.
- Which product characteristics should be prioritized in future vehicle development.

This project addresses these questions through analytical investigation of vehicle specifications and pricing data.

## Key Findings

- Higher horsepower vehicles generally command higher prices.
- Luxury and performance segments generate significantly higher average MSRPs.
- Fuel efficiency decreases as engine cylinder count increases.
- Vehicle category and brand positioning strongly influence pricing.
- Manufacturer reputation contributes significantly to market value perception.

---

## Business Recommendations

1. Focus product development on high-demand market categories.
2. Balance performance enhancements with fuel efficiency improvements.
3. Utilize feature-based pricing strategies.
4. Invest in technologies that improve fuel economy without sacrificing performance.
5. Target premium segments with advanced vehicle features.
